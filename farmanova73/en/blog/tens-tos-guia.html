Do You Have a Cough? Not All Coughs Are the Same: A Guide to Relieving It Successfully 🌿💨

A cough is one of our body's defence mechanisms, but we know it can be truly exhausting, especially at night. At the pharmacy, the first thing we do is "listen" to your cough to understand what you really need. An irritated throat is very different from a congested chest!

← Blog

1. The Dry Cough (or "barking cough"): Irritating and Persistent

This type has no mucus, is itchy and often makes the throat ache.

  • The approach: We need to "calm" and "hydrate".
  • Physiomance Mucus (Therascience): Ideal for protecting the mucous membranes and modulating inflammation from within.
  • Honey and plant-based syrups: Such as Mallow or Plantain, which create a protective film on the throat.
  • Extra tip: Avoid dry environments. Use a humidifier or place a bowl of water near the radiator.

2. The Productive Cough (with mucus): The Clearing Operation

Here the cough has a mission: to expel the mucus that has accumulated in the lungs. We should never suppress it, but help it!

  • The approach: We need to "loosen" the mucus so it comes out easily.
  • Ergybiol (Nutergia): Its copper and zinc content helps the immune system fight the infection causing the mucus.
  • Mucolytic syrups: Those that break down mucus chains (such as N-acetylcysteine-based or ivy extract ones).
  • Extra tip: Sliced onion on the bedside table? Yes, it works! Its sulphur compounds help open the airways.

🍎 "Anti-Cough" Diet: Eat to Breathe Better

  • Goodbye dairy (temporarily): Cow's milk can make mucus thicker and harder to expel.
  • Hot broths: Chicken or vegetable broth with onion and leek is a scientifically recognised natural anti-inflammatory.
  • Ginger and Lemon: Ginger is a powerful analgesic and lemon provides the Vitamin C needed for your immune system.
  • Maximum hydration: Water is the world's best mucolytic. Sip it slowly throughout the day.

🤔 Myths or Truths? Let's Bring Order to Your Natural Remedy Cabinet

At the pharmacy we hear many popular theories. What truth is there in what our grandmothers used to say?

1. "Sliced onion on the bedside table helps stop coughing."

PARTLY TRUE: Onion releases compounds called sulphoxides that have an analgesic and anti-inflammatory effect on the airways. It doesn't cure the infection, but it soothes irritation and helps you rest better if the cough is dry.

2. "Milk with honey is the best remedy for a cough."

IT DEPENDS: Honey is a fantastic natural antiseptic (especially for dry coughs). But be careful with milk! If you have a productive cough with mucus, cow's milk can make secretions thicker and harder to expel. In that case, a hot broth or herbal infusion is better.

3. "If I have a cough, I should take an antibiotic."

DANGEROUS MYTH: 90% of winter coughs are caused by viruses, and antibiotics only kill bacteria. Taking them without a prescription won't cure you, and will actually weaken your gut flora. That's why boosting your defences with Ergybiol (Nutergia) is a much smarter strategy.
